Two-Factor Authentication Now Required By Blue Lights

By
Eric
Facebook
Twitter
WhatsApp

    Amid increasing concerns for cybersecurity, the University of Florida announces its plan to incorporate DUO Two-Factor Authentication into its campus blue light phones. While these emergency devices act as a beacon for student safety and well being, they may also pose a potential threat to the schools data security.

    Safety remains UFs highest priority,

    Said UF spokesman Steve Orlando,

    Now this applies to our students, of course, but this more applies to the school.

    It is not our intention to inconvenience students. We understand it is no fun to use but DUO helps protect the university in case of an emergency - from hackers, or cyberpunks, or uh data-miners.

    The Crocodile asked the UPD whether or not accessing university information through a blue light breach was even possible.

    "Probably,"

    Said the UFPD.

    While enrolling in Two-Factor is simple enough, there are growing concerns about the time and mental clarity required to navigate the authentication process in an emergency situation.

    I didnt realize my potentially life-threatening emergency was also a threat to the University. Now I know,

    Pre-law senior Jane Levy told the Crocodile.

    The UFPD estimates that the authentication process should take no longer than 30 seconds to complete. 60 seconds if the student has poor service. This is assuming that the student has access to a mobile device.

    In case of an emergency, we recommend students run just a little faster to the blue light in order to make up for the time required to complete the authentication process.

    In the digital era, you can never be too safe. The school has expressed interest in further expanding Two-Factor Authentication to other campus vulnerabilities such as RTS lines, dining facilities, and on-campus crosswalks.
